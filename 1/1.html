II.        Cell structure and organelles

Each cell is made out of living material called protoplasm

Typical living cells are made out of 3 main parts:

  • Cell surface membrane (Plasma Membrane)
  • Cytoplasm
  • Nucleus

Note:

  • When drawing the nucleus, draw some lines to represent chromatin threads. Do not leave nucleus empty.

  • Label the cytoplasm, cell surface membrane, and nucleus next to each other and classify them together as protoplasm.

Good to know

Plasm                        plasma

∴ protoplasm                ‘prototype plasma’

Cell surface membrane (plasma membrane)

  1. Partially-permeable membrane
  2. Made out of proteins and lipids

(lipid bi-layer)
  1. Controls the movement of substances entering and leaving the cell
  2. Separates the internal environment (cytoplasm) from its surroundings

Cytoplasm
  1. Colloidal and viscous
  2. Mainly water with salts and sugars
  1. Fluid that contains many small structures called organelles
  2. Region where many chemical reactions take place

Nucleus

pl. Nuclei
  1. Filled with nucleoplasm
  2. Surrounded by a double-membrane
  3. Contains chromatin
  4. Has at least one nucleolus

Remember 3 Cs

  1. Contain genetic material
  2. Control cell activities
  3. Control cell division

Vacuole
  1. Fluid-filled space enclosed by a single membrane

Animal cells: many small temporary vacuoles

Plant cells: One large central vacuole

  • Enclosed by a P.P.M. called a tonoplast
  • Cell sap found within vacuole
  1. Stores substances (water, nutrients &c)
  2. Collect and remove waste and harmful substances

Animal cells

Stores food and water substances

Plant cells

Cell sap stores dissolved substances

  • Sugars
  • Mineral salts
  • Amino acids
  • Keep cytoplasm concentration constant
  • Maintain cell turgor, support the plant cell.

Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (RER)
  1. Originates from nuclear envelope
  2. Consists of cisternae – a network of flattened membranous sacs
  3. Appears rough due to attached ribosomes
  4. Single-membraned

Transports proteins synthesised by attached ribosomes to the golgi apparatus

Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum (SER)
  1. Originates from nuclear envelope
  1. Mostly connected to RER
  1. Consists mostly of tubules
  2. Single-membraned
  3. Does not have ribosomes attached
  1. Synthesises fats and steroids, and transports them to the Golgi apparatus
  2. Detoxifies harmful substances into harmless ones

Higher concentration

Liver cells – liver detoxes body
