I.  Particles in an atom

  • All elements are made out of atoms.
  • Each atom is made up of smaller sub-atomic particles
    • proton
    • neutron
    • electron

1.1  The subatomic particles

Nucleus proton 1 +1

  • Atoms are electrically neutral (have no overall charge)
  • For an atom, No. of protons = no. of electrons
  • Atoms contain the same amount of protons and neutrons.

1.2   Definitions

  • Nucleon number (mass number) — sum of the no. of protons and neutrons in an atom
  • Proton number (atomic number) — no. of protons in an atom
  • Atomic numbers are fixed for each atom. An atom is identified by its proton number. Different elements have different proton numbers.

II.  Representation of elements with symbols

  • Nucleon no. = no. of protons + no. of neutrons
  • Elements can be represented using only the nucleon number
    • e.g. chlorine-35, 35Cl

III. Arrangement of electrons in an atom

  • The way electrons are arranged in an atom is called its electronic structure or  electronic configuration.
  • They can be written as:
    • Diagrams
      • Atomic
      • Electronic
    • Electronic configuration number
      • e.g. 2,8,8

3.1   Justification for why electrons are organised in a specific way

  • Electrons are arranged in energy levels, called electron shells around the nucleus.
  • The closer the electrons are to the nucleus,the stronger the energy level.
  • Each 'energy level' can hold only hold a certain no. of electrons.

'Energy-level' rules for the first 20 elements of the Periodic Table:

To work out the arrangement of electrons:

  1. Find out the proton number of the element or atom
    1. No. of electrons = the proton number
      • Main rule —electrons always go to the nearest shell to the nucleus.
    2. If the shell is filled up, the electrons go to the next available shell and so on.

3.2    Valence electrons and the valence shell

Valence shell — the furthest electron shell from the nucleus
Valence electron —

  • Electrons in the valence shell
  • Determines chemical properties of an element

3.3    Arrangement of electrons and the periodic table

  • The position of an element is determined by its proton number.
  • All elements in the same group have the same number of valence electrons.
  • All elements in the same period have the same number of electron shells.
