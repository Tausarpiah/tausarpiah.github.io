I.    Introduction

  • Group 0 elements are called noble gases. They are extremely unreactive / inert.
  • Atoms of noble gases have completely full valence shells.
    • This makes them very stable.
  • Most elements, apart from Group 0 / 18 elements, react with one another so that they achieve the 'noble gas structure'.
    • i.e. — have a full valence shell.
  • These reactive elements react by:
    • losing electrons;
    • gaining electrons;
    • sharing electrons.
  • The force of attraction holding atoms together is called a chemical bond.

1.1    Ions

Definition: An ion is a charged particle formed when an atom loses or gains electrons.

All ions have the electronic structure (configuration) of a noble gas (i.e. — full valence shell).

1.2    Ionic bonding

  • Formed from a reaction between metals and non-metals.
  • Metals tend to lose electrons to non-metal atoms.
  • Only valence electrons are involved in bonding.

An ionic bond is the electrostatic force of attraction between +ve ions and -ve ions.
