Assets

How much do I have?

Assets are anything valuable that a company owns, whether it's land, equipment, buildings or 'intellectual property'.

So long something has value and is owned by you, it is an asset.

Common asset types

  • Accounts recievable: Payments that clients and customers owe
  • Cash: Money in the bank account.
  • Property and Equipment (P&E): buildings/tools needed for operation.

Classification of assets:

Assets are generally divided into two catergories:
  • Current assets: Cash, and anything that can be relatively converted into cash.
  • Fixed assets: Land, trade-marks, and the intangible 'value' of a brand.

Liabilities

How much do I owe?

Liabilities are any debts your company has or owes.

If someone has promised to pay someone in the future, and hasn't paid them yet, that’s a liability.

Common liabilities

  • Accounts payable: payments owed to suppliers.
  • Bank loans: principal owed to investors (in this case, the bank is the investor).
  • Salaries & wages payable: Wages and wages to be paid later.

Classification of liabilities

There are two main kinds of liabilities:
  1. Current: debts within 1 year/12 months
  2. Non-current / Future: debts ranging beyond 1 year/12 months.

Equities

How much is left over?

Equity is how much value is left over once you’ve totalled up everything valuable that you have, and subtracted everything you owe to your creditors.

For many smaller owners, equity is the essential net worth of their business.

Equity equation

Assets - Liabilities = Equity

Types of equity

  • For sole proprietorship, equity is usually owner's equity.
  • For corporations (i.e. public liability companies), it is called shareholder's equity.

Equity equation

This whole thing can be summed up by:

Assets - Liabilities = Equities
